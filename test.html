Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences

Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences comments:

For ICS course updates, visit the ICS Enrollment Restrictions webpage:

Undergraduate courses: http://ics.uci.edu/course-enrollment-restrictions/

Graduate courses: http://ics.uci.edu/academics/graduate-academic-advising/course-updates/

It is incumbent on the student to check the restriction status and seating availability of a course before adding or attempting to add or change their discussion/lab section enrollment.

COURSE PREREQUISITES ARE STRICTLY ENFORCED. The School of ICS WILL DROP students who do not meet the required course prerequisite(s). These drops may take place at any time after grades are posted until Friday of Week 2. Note that meeting the prerequisites for a course does not guarantee a seat in that course.

WAITLIST: Students on the waitlist for an ICS course should be in communication with that course's instructor to arrange for access to course materials and submission of any assignments while on the waitlist. Being on the waitlist or completing coursework DOES NOT guarantee a seat in the course.

DEADLINES TO ADD, DROP, OR CHANGE GRADE OPTION FOR ALL SCHOOL OF ICS COURSES

5:00pm on Friday of Week 1: Courses with an early deadline to add/drop/change grade option are noted with a comment in the Schedule of Classes. Permission from the instructor and an authorization code will be required after Week 1 to add or drop these courses.

5:00pm on Friday of Week 2: The deadline to add/drop/change grade option in WebReg is 5:00pm on Friday, October 10, 2025. The Bren School of ICS strictly adheres to and enforces this deadline for undergraduate and graduate courses.

ADDING, DROPPING, OR CHANGING GRADE OPTION AFTER WEEK 2: Students must submit an Enrollment Exception request online through StudentAccess. Exception requests will only be approved for extenuating and documented circumstances outside of the student's control. For more information on ICS policies: http://ics.uci.edu/academics/undergraduate-programs/majors-minors/undergraduate-student-policies/

GRADUATE ENROLLMENT IN UNDERGRADUATE COURSES: Graduate students seeking to enroll in an ICS undergraduate level course must submit a request to ICS Undergraduate Students Affairs. Priority for seating in undergraduate courses is given first to undergraduate students. Unauthorized students found enrolled in undergraduate courses WILL BE DROPPED by ICS Student Affairs.

UNDERGRADUATE ENROLLMENT IN GRADUATE COURSES: Undergraduate students seeking to enroll in an ICS graduate level course must obtain a written permission from the instructor before submitting a request for an authorization to ICS Graduate Student Affairs. Priority for seating in graduate courses is given first to graduate students. Graduate level courses taken as an undergraduate will not count toward a graduate degree program.

DCE CONCURRENT ENROLLMENT: Only courses that show as "OPEN" in the Schedule of Classes may be requested. Approval of requests is contingent on seating availability and prerequisite eligibility. For more information: http://ics.uci.edu/academics/undergraduate-academic-advising/concurrent-enrollment/
Information and Computer Science

Information and Computer Science department comments:

RESTRICTIONS: Most I&C SCI courses will be initially restricted to majors in the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences ONLY. Some courses will be additionally restricted to specific majors or level(s) of standing. For restriction details and update timeline: http://ics.uci.edu/course-enrollment-restrictions/

WAITLIST: Students on the waitlist for an ICS course should be in communication with that course's instructor to arrange for access to course materials and submission of any assignments while on the waitlist. Being on the waitlist or completing coursework DOES NOT guarantee a seat in the course.

DISCUSSIONS & LABS: Discussion and lab sections begin meeting AFTER the first lecture. Always check for messages from your instructors before the start of instruction.

Some lab sections are scheduled in laptop instructional labs, ICS 364A, ICS2 162, or ICS2 170. Students enrolled in these labs are required to bring a personal laptop to class meetings.

B or X Restricted Courses: Courses with a B or X restriction require an authorization code from the instructor to enroll.
